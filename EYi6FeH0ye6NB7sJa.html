Detailed Compliance Report: The City Bank Limited

Jurisdiction: Bangladesh

Report Date: 2023-06-20

Executive Summary

The City Bank Limited, one of the leading private commercial banks in Bangladesh, demonstrates a moderate risk profile with partial compliance to regulatory standards. The bank has shown improvements in its compliance practices but still faces challenges in certain areas, particularly in relation to PEP exposure and transaction monitoring.

Risk Score: 67/100 (Moderate Risk)

Compliance Status: Partial Compliance

Key Risk Factors

PEP Exposure

Medium Risk: Several board members have political connections, potentially increasing the risk of conflicts of interest and requiring enhanced due diligence.

AML Procedures

Moderate Risk: While AML procedures are in place, there is room for improvement in transaction monitoring systems to detect and report suspicious activities more effectively.

Regulatory Compliance

Moderate Risk: Recent minor violations in reporting procedures have been noted, indicating a need for stronger internal controls and staff training.

Detailed Analysis

1. Corporate Governance

The bank's board composition includes individuals with political connections, which while not uncommon in the region, does present elevated risks. Enhanced monitoring and strict conflict of interest policies are recommended.

2. AML/CFT Framework

The bank has implemented standard AML/CFT policies, but the effectiveness of its transaction monitoring system is below industry best practices. Upgrading to more sophisticated AI-driven monitoring tools is advised.

3. Regulatory Compliance

Minor reporting violations in the past year suggest a need for improved internal processes and staff training. The bank has been cooperative with regulators in addressing these issues.

4. Customer Due Diligence

CDD procedures are generally adequate but could be enhanced, especially for high-risk customers and PEPs. Implementation of a risk-based approach to CDD is recommended.

5. Sanctions Screening

The bank employs automated sanctions screening tools, but there have been instances of false positives affecting operational efficiency. Refinement of screening parameters is suggested.

Recommendations

  1. Enhance transaction monitoring systems with AI-driven analytics to improve detection of suspicious activities.
  2. Implement stricter controls and oversight for PEP-related accounts and transactions.
  3. Conduct a comprehensive review of regulatory reporting processes and provide targeted training to relevant staff.
  4. Upgrade CDD procedures, particularly for high-risk customers, incorporating more dynamic risk assessment models.
  5. Refine sanctions screening parameters to reduce false positives while maintaining effectiveness.

Next Steps

SynComply recommends a follow-up assessment in 6 months to evaluate the implementation of suggested improvements and reassess the bank's risk profile and compliance status.
